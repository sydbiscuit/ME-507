Introduction

Parkinson’s is a neuromuscular disease that causes involuntary tremors in patients. Tremors adversely affect a person’s quality of life, causing poor motor control in daily activities, frustration, and embarrassment. Along with a second team in the class (Tremor Mug 2) who is sponsored by the primary stakeholder for this design, Richmond McGuire Veterans Hospital, we are designing a tremor mug that will help people with tremors regain confidence for a simple task: holding a container full of liquid without spilling. Current tremor reduction products often require two hands to use, are not visually appealing, or are simply ineffective. Although the final form of the product would ideally be lighter in weight and more compact, for our prototype development we wanted to design with a microcontroller that both team members had readily available, such as a Nucleo L476RG. The microcontroller selection caused the PCB and remaining hardware to be too large to fit into a standard sized coffee mug. Consequently, the “mug” was upgraded to a larger, commercialized Brita filter jug, as seen below.
